Description

Epigenomics is an emerging frontier of science. This short course will cover current topics in epigenomics, including modern epigenomics technologies and their data analysis in the context of integration of big bio-data.

    3. High-throughput sequencing
  2. Epigenetic mechanisms (Apr 18)

  3. Epigenome and life (Apr 20)

  4. Technology in modern epigenomics (Apr 22)

  5. From genome to epigenome to genome 4D (Apr 25)
    1. Chromatin biology

Lab assignment (optional)

Instructor will design a small project involving using real life, high-throughput sequencing data to analyze patterns of the epigenome in the context of normal human cell type differentiation. The goal of the project is to walk through a typical data analysis pipeline in epigenomics study, from mapping reads, to calling peaks, to compute correlations and other statistics of the data, and to visualize the results. Everyone will start with raw reads, and can be provided any intermediate results as input for the next step. We can define the whole problem on the first day and allow students to carry it as a project to different levels.

Workshop (optional)

Instructor will present a workshop to introduce epigenomic resources including the UCSC Genome Browser and the WashU Epigenome Browser. Students will learn how to access, download, analyze, and visualize epigenomic data using these tools.

Journal club (optional)

Students will be assembled into small groups, and assigned a classic paper in the field. Students will then present a journal club and lead discussions. Instructor will provide feedback.
